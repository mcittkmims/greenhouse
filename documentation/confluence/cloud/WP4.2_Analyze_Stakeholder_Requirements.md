## Overview

This document presents the analysis and MoSCoW classification of stakeholder requirements for the Greenhouse Management System (GMS). The requirements in this document are derived directly from WP4.1 — Develop Stakeholder Requirements, which sourced them from four primary inputs: the stakeholder survey conducted in February 2026 (18 respondents), stakeholder workshop discussions, direct stakeholder interviews, and findings from the technology research phase. A second set of requirements, extracted specifically from open-ended survey responses, is presented separately in the lower section of the table.
Each entry in the table includes the following columns:
- **Category** — the functional area the requirement belongs to.
- **Stakeholder Requirement** — a precise, verifiable statement of what the system must or should do.
- **US (User Story)** — a role-based expression of the requirement from the user's perspective.
- **JS (Job Story)** — a context-driven expression of the requirement focused on the situation and motivation.
- **Classification** — MoSCoW priority assigned based on stakeholder evidence and project constraints.
- **Rationale** — the evidence-based justification for the assigned classification.

## Stakeholder Requirements Review

### Iteration 1 — Peer Review of Should Have and Could Have Requirements

**Date:** March 5, 2026 **Reviewers:** Alexei Maxim, Vremere Adrian, Cojocaru Daniel, Rațeeva Daria, Mutruc Victoria **Scope:** Should Have and Could Have tier requirements.
All five team members independently reviewed the full requirements table, assessing each entry for clarity, completeness, accuracy, and alignment with project goals. Findings were then consolidated.

#### Issues Found

| # | Found By | Requirement | Issue | Recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| R1 | Rațeeva Daria | Affordability — demonstrate cost savings | The requirement states the system "should demonstrate clear cost savings" but defines no measurement mechanism or baseline. Without a defined output, it cannot be verified at the end of the pilot. | Define a concrete measurement approach: the system should log water and energy consumption and compare it against a manually-set baseline established at pilot start. |
| R2 | Alexei Maxim | Historical Data and Reports — store and view trends | The requirement does not specify a data retention period. Without a defined horizon, the storage architecture cannot be properly planned and the requirement cannot be tested. | Specify a minimum retention period (e.g., 90 days) to enable meaningful seasonal trend analysis and make the requirement verifiable. |
| R3 | Cojocaru Daniel | Alerts and Notifications — SMS/push | The requirement triggers notifications "when critical thresholds are exceeded" but does not define a severity tier. Without distinguishing critical from informational events, all threshold breaches may trigger off-screen alerts, causing alert fatigue. | Define a severity classification — e.g., informational events are shown in-dashboard only, while critical events (actuator failure, extreme temperature) trigger SMS/push. |
| R4 | Vremere Adrian | Temperature and Humidity — separate day/night alerts | The requirement addresses only the heat/frost distinction. Rapid rate-of-change events (e.g., sudden temperature drop of 5°C in under 10 minutes) are not covered, leaving operators without warning during fast-moving weather events. | Confirm whether rate-of-change alerting is in scope or explicitly defer it, to avoid an implicit gap in the alerting architecture. |
| R5 | Mutruc Victoria | Historical Data and Reports — export for compliance | The requirement references "record-keeping and compliance" but does not specify required export formats or data fields. Agronomists and regulatory bodies may expect specific columns such as timestamp, sensor ID, and measurement unit. | Clarify that the minimum export must include Excel/CSV compatibility with timestamp and sensor ID fields, consistent with explicit survey requests from technical respondents. |

#### Refinement Augmentation

During the implementation pass, the team confirmed that the affordability requirements collectively address both the adoption barrier (Must Have — system must be affordable) and the value demonstration need (Should Have — demonstrate savings). No new requirements were added; the distinction between these two complementary concerns is sufficiently captured by the separate table entries and will be formalised with independent acceptance criteria in the SRS phase.

### Release — Stakeholder Requirements Elicitation

The Stakeholder Requirements table above represents the finalised output of the elicitation and analysis process. Requirements were sourced from the stakeholder survey (18 respondents, February 2026), stakeholder workshop discussions, direct stakeholder interviews, and technology research. They were classified using MoSCoW, validated through User Story and Job Story pairs, and justified by rationale drawn from primary research data.
The Should Have and Could Have tiers were subject to a dedicated peer review with five recommendations raised across all team members. No reclassifications were required; all recommendations were either resolved in-place or logged for carry-over into the System Requirements Specification. The table is considered complete and stable for handoff to the SRS work package.
